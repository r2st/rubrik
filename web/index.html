What do these labels mean?
Sentiment score
1–5 scale carried in each meeting's summary. 1 = strongly negative, 3 = neutral, 5 = strongly positive. Aggregations show the arithmetic mean across the matching meetings.
Call type
support (Support Case prefix), external (customer-facing call), internal (internal meeting). Classified by regex on the meeting title.
Meeting purpose
One of 11 ordered buckets — Account Management, Discovery, Demo, QBR, Renewal, Pricing, Onboarding, Roadmap, Escalation, Internal Sync, Other. First-match rules applied to the title.
Product area
Multi-label tags by keyword bag: Detect, Comply, Protect, Identity, General. A meeting can carry several. General is the catch-all when no product keyword fires.
Weekly trend
Mean sentiment per ISO week, split by call type. The red band marks the Detect outage window — the dominant noise source in this view.

Drill-down

What do these labels mean?
Tier (🔴 / 🟡 / 🟢)
Bucketed risk level. 🔴 high = score ≥ 0.40, 🟡 medium = 0.25–0.40, 🟢 low = below 0.25. Thresholds and weights are tunable in /admin (settings → category risk).
Risk score
Composite in [0, 1] where higher = more at risk. Weighted sum of three normalised signals: 0.5 × sentiment_gap + 0.3 × churn_signals + 0.2 × negative_pivots.
Avg sentiment
Mean of this customer's per-meeting sentiment scores (1–5).
Min sentiment
Lowest single-meeting sentiment in the customer's history — surfaces the worst call, not the average.
Meetings
Count of distinct meetings tagged to this customer.
Churn signals
Total key moments tagged churn_signal by the source data (e.g., "considering competitor", "cancelling renewal"). Direct verbal signal.
Avg max-drop
Average within-meeting sentiment pivot — the trajectory signal. A meeting that started fine and went south late shows up here even if its summary score looks OK.

What do these labels mean?
Affected meetings
Meetings where the transcript or summary references the Detect outage in the affected window. Identified by keyword + title patterns (e.g., "URGENT: Detect", post-mortem references).
Sentiment delta
Affected-meeting average sentiment minus baseline (unaffected) average. A larger negative number = the outage dragged the conversation lower than the rest of the period.
Blast radius
Share of all meetings in the window that carry an outage reference. Captures how far the incident leaked into otherwise unrelated calls (renewals, demos, internal syncs).
Direct incident-response timeline
Meetings where the title itself signals direct response (URGENT, Escalation, Post-mortem). The narrow shape of the response, not the broader leak.

Pick a meeting

What do these labels mean?
Sentiment trajectory
Per-sentence sentiment numerically encoded (positive=+1, neutral=0, negative=−1) and bucketed into 10 equal time slices. Surfaces within-call dynamics the meeting-level summary score hides.
Max drop
Largest negative delta between consecutive trajectory buckets — the steepest mid-meeting fall. A meeting can end on a high note yet have an alarming max-drop earlier.
End − start
Final-bucket sentiment minus opening-bucket sentiment. Positive = the call recovered; negative = it deteriorated.
Share negative
Fraction of sentences labelled negative. A meeting with low share-negative but a deep max-drop = a brief, sharp friction event.
Action items
Owner-tagged tasks extracted from the call summary. The Customers and Insights pages aggregate these to find owner-level bottlenecks.
Key moments
Tagged events from the source summary (e.g., churn_signal, positive_feedback, blocker). Drives the churn-signal count in the risk score.

Content clusters

What do these labels mean?
Cluster
A group of meetings whose transcripts share a vocabulary fingerprint. Each meeting belongs to exactly one cluster; the goal is surfacing latent themes that cross the rule-based call-type / purpose taxonomy.
k
Number of clusters. Chosen automatically by sweeping k ∈ [4, 10] and picking the value with the highest silhouette score. No hardcoded k.
Silhouette
Quality measure in [-1, 1]. Higher = clusters are well-separated. Low values (~0.05–0.15) are normal for conversational text — vocabulary overlaps heavily across topics.
Top terms
The TF-IDF terms with the highest weight at each cluster's centroid. Read these as "what makes this cluster different from the others", not as a topic label.
Size
Number of meetings assigned to the cluster. Very small clusters (1–2 meetings) are usually outliers and should be inspected manually before drawing conclusions.
Why no labels
KMeans gives membership, not human-readable names. We surface the top terms instead so an operator can label the cluster after the fact based on context.
